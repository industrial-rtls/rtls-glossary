Data Visualization

Graphical representation of RTLS data making complex tracking information easily understandable. Common visualizations include facility maps with real-time positions, heat maps, trajectory plots, timeline views, and dashboards with KPI gauges. Effective visualizations use appropriate chart types, clear visual hierarchy, intuitive color coding, and interactive capabilities. Bridges the gap between complex systems and non-technical users.

Graphical representation of RTLS location and operational data enabling intuitive understanding of complex patterns. Industrial RTLS visualization types include: (1) Real-time facility maps - 2D or 3D facility layouts showing current positions of all tracked assets, color-coded by status, updated sub-second. (2) Heatmaps - density visualizations showing high-traffic areas, dwell time concentrations, or congestion zones aggregated over hours/days/weeks. (3) Spaghetti diagrams - path traces showing movement routes, revealing inefficient workflows and unnecessary travel. (4) Timeline charts - Gantt-style views showing asset progression through process steps over time. (5) Statistical dashboards - KPI trends, histograms of cycle times, utilization gauges. (6) 3D visualizations - perspective views useful for multi-level facilities or complex equipment arrangements. Effective visualization design principles include: appropriate aggregation level (showing 1000 assets individually is overwhelming, zones/heatmaps more useful), time window selection (real-time for operations, historical for analysis), color schemes (color-blind friendly, sufficient contrast), and interactive capabilities (zoom, filter, drill-down). Modern RTLS platforms support customizable dashboards allowing different stakeholder groups to view relevant metrics without technical expertise.
