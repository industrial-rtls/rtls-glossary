The movement, storage, and control of materials throughout manufacturing and warehousing. RTLS optimizes material handling by providing real-time visibility, optimizing transport routes, and identifying bottlenecks. Directly impacts operational efficiency and costs.

Material Handling

Physical movement and storage of materials within facility, including equipment (forklifts, conveyors, cranes), processes (receiving, storage, picking, staging, shipping), and optimization approaches enhanced by RTLS visibility. Material handling performance metrics enabled by RTLS: equipment utilization (productive time percentage, targets 55-75% accounting for breaks and charging), travel distance (meters per shift, with 20-40% reduction typical through optimization), cycle times (time per handling task, with high variability indicating process inconsistency), congestion hotspots (areas with frequent equipment conflicts), and safety incidents (near-misses and collisions detected through proximity monitoring). Material handling optimization approaches: (1) Slotting optimization - positioning frequently-moved items near shipping to minimize travel (ABC analysis with A items closest), enabled by RTLS data showing actual pick frequencies and travel patterns. (2) Zone assignment - dedicating equipment to specific facility zones reducing travel and congestion. (3) Task interleaving - combining outbound picks with inbound putaways on same trip eliminating empty travel (requires real-time position visibility). (4) Dynamic routing - directing equipment along optimal paths considering current congestion (GPS-like navigation inside facility). (5) Fleet optimization - right-sizing equipment quantities based on actual utilization (typical finding: 15-25% equipment reduction possible through better allocation). RTLS-driven material handling improvements typically achieve: 15-25% labor productivity increase (reduced search time, optimized routing), 20-35% equipment cost reduction (fleet right-sizing through utilization data), 10-20% throughput increase (eliminating bottlenecks and congestion), 40-60% safety incident reduction (collision avoidance and proximity warnings), and 25-40% facility space reduction (optimized layouts based on actual flow patterns). ROI typically 12-24 months for material handling RTLS implementations.
