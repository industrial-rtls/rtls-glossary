Technical Parameters

Meter-Level Accuracy

Positioning accuracy determining positions within 1-3 meters of true location. Sufficient for many industrial applications including asset tracking, warehouse management, and personnel monitoring. Commonly achieved with BLE systems or Wi-Fi positioning.

Positioning performance achieving location errors in 1-5 meter range, typical for BLE, Wi-Fi, and lower-tier positioning systems. Meter-level accuracy suitable for applications not requiring precise positioning: zone-based tracking (confirming asset in general area rather than exact position), personnel location (finding worker within department or area), low-resolution heatmaps (identifying high-traffic zones), and asset finding (narrowing search area to manageable zone). Technologies achieving meter-level accuracy: BLE RSSI-based positioning (1-5 meters typical in industrial environments), Wi-Fi fingerprinting or RSSI (3-15 meters), active RFID (zone-level only, 5-20 meter zones), and magnetic positioning (1-3 meters).

Meter-level accuracy advantages: lower cost (BLE tags $15-40 vs. UWB $50-150), lower infrastructure density (1 gateway per 1000-2000 m² vs. 1 UWB anchor per 400-1000 m²), existing infrastructure leverage (using existing Wi-Fi access points), and adequate for many applications (zone tracking, occupancy counting, asset locating in large facilities). Many industrial RTLS deployments use hybrid accuracy: meter-level BLE/Wi-Fi for general warehouse tracking, centimeter-level UWB for production floor where precision required, optimizing cost-performance balance across facility.

Typical decision framework: production assembly requiring component positioning needs centimeter-level (UWB), warehouse put-away/picking needs 0.5-2 meter (BLE AoA or Wi-Fi), large outdoor yards tolerate 2-5 meter (BLE RSSI or GPS), simple zone detection accepts 5+ meter zones (active RFID). Meter-level accuracy market segment growing as BLE 5.1 AoA systems mature, offering 0.5-2 meter accuracy at price points between basic RSSI (3-8 meter) and premium UWB (10-30 cm), filling gap for applications needing better than zone-level but not requiring centimeter precision.
