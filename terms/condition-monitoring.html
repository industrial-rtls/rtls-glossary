Condition Monitoring

Use of RTLS-integrated sensors to track environmental conditions or asset health. Beyond position tracking, modern RTLS tags integrate sensors monitoring: temperature (critical for cold chain, heat stress), humidity (moisture-sensitive materials), vibration/shock (detecting impacts during transport), tilt/orientation (ensuring proper handling of sensitive equipment), light exposure (verifying storage conditions), or door-open sensors (monitoring container security). In industrial applications, condition monitoring enables: automated alerts when temperature-sensitive materials leave acceptable ranges, documentation of proper handling for regulatory compliance, predictive maintenance by monitoring equipment vibration patterns, quality assurance by tracking environmental exposure throughout production, and inventory management by confirming proper storage conditions. Sensor data typically transmitted with position updates at reduced frequency (every 1-10 minutes vs. 1-second position updates) to conserve battery. Sensor integration increases tag cost 20-50% and reduces battery life 20-40%. Most valuable for high-value or sensitive materials where condition violations have significant cost impacts.
