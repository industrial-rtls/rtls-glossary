Safety & Security

Proximity Warning

A subset of proximity alert specifically focused on safety applications. Warns operators or workers when dangerous proximity conditions exist such as forklifts approaching pedestrians or equipment nearing hazards. Graduated warnings increase in urgency as distance decreases.

Alert or signal provided to personnel or equipment operators indicating nearby hazard, specifically focused on communication of danger rather than broader proximity detection. Multi-level warnings: advisory (potential risk, 5-10 meters), caution (developing risk, 3-5 meters), critical (immediate risk, 1-3 meters) with escalating urgency. (2) Hazardous zone warnings - alerting workers approaching restricted or dangerous areas (high-voltage equipment, moving machinery, chemical storage, confined spaces). (3) Equipment proximity warnings - alerting operators when approaching fixed obstacles preventing collisions and property damage. Optimal: 2-5 seconds warning time accounting for human reaction (0.5-1.5 seconds) plus action execution. (2) Reliability - consistent detection and alerting with high detection rate (>95%), low false negative rate (<2%), acceptable false positive rate (<5%). (3) Clarity - unambiguous meaning about threat type, location, and required action. (4) Consistency - predictable behavior building user trust.

Typical 200-person warehouse implementing proximity warnings reduces vehicle-pedestrian incidents 50-70%. (2) Manufacturing - protecting workers from mobile equipment and fixed hazards. (3) Construction - alerting workers to heavy equipment. (4) Mining - protecting personnel from mining vehicles. (5) Ports and logistics - preventing accidents around cargo handling equipment. Proximity warning benefits: (1) Accident prevention - 40-70% reduction in proximity-related incidents. (2) Severity reduction - incidents typically less severe due to reduced speed. (3) Safety culture - constant safety awareness. (4) Near-miss visibility - capturing near-miss events providing leading indicators. (5) Compliance - demonstrating proactive safety approach.

Proximity warning costs: $30-80 per pedestrian badge, $150-400 per vehicle installation, plus RTLS infrastructure.

Typical facility: $50-150k total investment.
