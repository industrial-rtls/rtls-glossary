Metrics & KPIs

Turnaround Time

The time required to complete a process from start to finish, often used in logistics for vehicle or shipment processing times. RTLS automatically measures turnaround times providing objective data for analysis and improvement. Critical metric for dock operations and transportation efficiency.

Turnaround time in industrial operations refers to the total elapsed time required to complete a process, cycle, or transaction from initiation to conclusion. RTLS enables automated turnaround time measurement by: defining process start and end zones (eg., dock entry and exit zones, work cell entry and exit), detecting when assets enter and exit these zones through position tracking, recording timestamps for zone transitions, calculating elapsed time between entry and exit, and aggregating data across multiple cycles for statistical analysis (mean, median, standard deviation, percentile distributions).

Typical dock turnarounds range from 30 minutes to 3+ hours depending on load size, facility efficiency, and operational factors. Analytics derived from turnaround time data include: average turnaround time providing baseline performance metrics, turnaround time distribution showing variability and consistency (tight distributions indicate consistent processes, wide distributions suggest inconsistency), trend analysis identifying improving or degrading performance over time, comparative analysis benchmarking across facilities, shifts, or operators identifying best practices, and outlier identification detecting unusually long turnarounds indicating problems requiring investigation. Quantified benefits include: 20-40% turnaround time reductions through process improvements identified via data analysis, 15-30% capacity increases from faster turnarounds enabling more transactions per day, 10-20% cost reductions from improved resource utilization, and improved customer satisfaction from faster, more predictable service. Integration with operational systems enhances turnaround time insights: correlating turnaround times with workload (transaction size, complexity) to normalize performance, linking to resource allocation data (staffing levels, equipment availability) to optimize capacity, and feeding scheduling systems with actual performance data improving planning accuracy.
