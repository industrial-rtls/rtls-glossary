Positioning Accuracy

Measure of how precisely RTLS determines object positions relative to true positions, fundamental performance specification. Accuracy expressed as: mean error (average distance between reported and true positions), percentile error (eg., 90th percentile: 90% of measurements within specified error), or error distribution showing error characteristics. Critical to specify conditions: static versus dynamic (moving tags typically show 20-50% worse accuracy), line-of-sight versus non-line-of-sight (NLoS degrades accuracy 30-100%+), and confidence level (50th percentile/median, 90th percentile, 95th percentile - vendor specifications often cite median which is optimistic compared to 90th percentile). Technology accuracy ranges: (1) UWB - 10-30 cm (90th percentile, good conditions), 20-50 cm (industrial environments with some NLoS). (2) BLE AoA - 0.5-2 meters (good conditions), 1-3 meters (realistic industrial). (3) BLE RSSI - 3-8 meters (highly variable, environment-dependent). (4) Wi-Fi fingerprinting - 3-15 meters (depends on fingerprint database quality). (5) Active RFID - zone-level only (5-20 meter zones). (6) GPS - 3-10 meters (outdoors, civilian signals). Accuracy requirements vary by application: (1) Centimeter-level (5-30 cm) - AGV guidance, robotic picking, precision assembly tracking. (2) Decimeter-level (30-100 cm) - forklift tracking, collision avoidance, work cell tracking. (3) Meter-level (1-5 meters) - warehouse zone tracking, personnel location, large area asset tracking. (4) Room/zone-level (5-20 meters) - basic asset location, presence detection, occupancy counting. Example: vendor claiming 15 cm accuracy might mean median in ideal LoS conditions, while 90th percentile in actual environment might be 40-60 cm. Understanding accuracy critical for setting requirements, evaluating vendors, managing expectations, and troubleshooting performance issues.
